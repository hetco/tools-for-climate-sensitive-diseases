In this review and synthesis, we had two objectives

1 — To Identify Tools

We scoped the landscape of models addressing climate-sensitive infectious diseases, with an eye to identifying the current available set of tools for public health decision makers.

2 — To Understand Expert Perspectives on Tools

We engaged with researchers and policy stakeholders at key organizations in the fields of climate and infectious disease, and conducted semi-structured interviews to understand their perspectives on the software tools landscape.

We aimed to identify tools that :

  • Incorporate both climate inputs and epidemiological information.
  • Produce an output as a prediction or indicator of disease risk in one package.
  • Are transparently described and validated.
  • Are named, for future searching and versioning.
  • Are accessible - code is published, or available on a code repository, a web platform, or other.
